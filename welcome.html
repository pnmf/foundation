Pratiman-Neema Memorial Health Institute (PNMHI; formally known as the Pratiman-Neema Vocational Training Institute) came to existence in 2008 as a non-profit academic institute, one of a handful of non-profit academic institutes in Nepal. It has aimed to deliver higher education as well as to produce skilled manpower with an eye on the empowerment of the underprivileged population. The institute is in Bhairahawa, the southern plain of Nepal near Lumbini, the birth place of Buddha.

The institute began with a program in health related field offering a three-year associate degree in Medical Laboratory Technician. A total of 211 students have been enrolled in this institute. The first batch completed their study in 2011 from this institute with a 100% job placement, and so far 80 students have received their degrees. The institute has been looking to expand its program not only to provide Bachelor's and Master's degrees in the health related fields but also in other areas such as agro-forestry and sustainability, business and economics, and engineering and environmental sciences.

The institute (PNMHI) as an academic entity was established by a non-profit organization, Pratiman-Neema Memorial Foundation (PNMF). The PNMF donated land for the new campus building near a river bank. In 2008, the PNMF submitted a development grant proposal and finally received the funding ($400,000) approval in 2011 for the institute’s new campus building. The institute’s new campus building is under construction, and will be in use very soon. In the near future, the institute plans to expand its academic programs with a goal to be a full-fledged multidisciplinary college. One of its signature programs will be an innovation lab that will be designed to promote creativity, innovation, technology, and entrepreneurship.
